Microplastics (MPs) are captured in wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) sludge at over 90% of the particles entering the plant. This contaminated sludge is usually land-applied or incinerated, spreading microplastics into soils and food chains, threatening ecosystem health and crop safety.

Economic Impact: MP contamination forces municipalities to pay for costly sludge disposal (landfilling or incineration) instead of generating revenue by selling clean biosolids to agriculture‚Äîrepresenting a significant lost opportunity.

Phase 2: Pilot-Scale Testing

Expansion based on pilot results. Full integration with existing WWTP infrastructure.
